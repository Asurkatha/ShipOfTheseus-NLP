Across datasets, Human-authored originals show POS cosine 0.974 +/- 0.012 under ChatGPT and 0.892 +/- 0.047 under Dipper (High), while LLM-authored originals show 0.967 +/- 0.006 and 0.880 +/- 0.027, respectively. If the Human bars are lower, then POS distributions shift more for Human text; if the LLM bars are lower, then paraphrasers move LLM-authored text further from its starting style. A plausible explanation is that LLM-generated T0 text already occupies an AI-like stylistic basin, so another LLM paraphraser may preserve its POS profile more easily than it preserves the broader syntactic range seen in Human writing. That makes POS drift partly a measure of how far the starting text already aligns with the paraphraser’s preferred distribution.
